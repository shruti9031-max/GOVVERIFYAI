How It Works

A comprehensive breakdown of our AI-powered verification process from upload to verdict

5-Step Verification Process

Every document goes through rigorous multi-layer analysis powered by artificial intelligence

1

Document Upload

Users can submit government notices through multiple channels - file upload, text paste, or URL input. Our system accepts various formats including PDFs, images, and text documents.

  • Support for PDF, JPG, PNG, DOCX formats
  • Direct text paste for messages and forwards
  • URL verification for web-based notices
  • End-to-end encryption during transmission

  • Tesseract OCR engine for text extraction
  • Support for Hindi and English languages
  • Metadata extraction (creation date, author, etc.)
  • Logo and seal detection using computer vision

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our verification process

How accurate is the AI detection system? ‚ñº

Our AI system has been tested on over 10,000 documents and achieves 99.2% detection accuracy. It has a 98.5% true positive rate (correctly identifying fakes) and only 0.8% false positive rate (incorrectly flagging authentic notices). The system is continuously learning and improving with each verification.

What file formats are supported? ‚ñº

GovVerify AI supports PDF documents, image files (JPG, PNG, JPEG), Word documents (DOCX), and plain text. You can also paste text directly or provide URLs to online notices. Maximum file size is 10MB per upload.

How long does verification take? ‚ñº

The average verification time is 2.8 seconds. This includes document upload, OCR extraction, AI analysis, database cross-checking, and report generation. Complex documents with multiple pages may take slightly longer (5-10 seconds).

Is my data secure and private? ‚ñº

Absolutely. All uploaded documents are encrypted using AES-256 encryption during transmission and storage. Documents are automatically deleted from our servers after 24 hours. We never share your data with third parties, and you can verify documents anonymously without creating an account.

Can it detect all types of fake notices? ‚ñº

Our system is trained to detect common fraud patterns including fake yojana schemes, pension scams, subsidy frauds, and official-looking documents with forged seals. While we achieve high accuracy, we always recommend users verify critical information through official government channels as an additional precaution.

What languages are supported? ‚ñº

Currently, GovVerify AI supports English and Hindi, which covers the majority of government communications in India. We are working on adding support for more regional languages including Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, Marathi, and Gujarati in future updates.

How does the trust score work? ‚ñº

The trust score (0-100) is calculated based on multiple factors: presence in official databases (40%), language pattern analysis (25%), visual authenticity (20%), and metadata verification (15%). Scores 70-100 indicate likely authentic, 30-70 are suspicious and need manual verification, and 0-30 suggest likely fraudulent notices.

What should I do if a notice is flagged as fake? ‚ñº

If a notice is flagged as fraudulent: (1) Do not share personal or financial information, (2) Do not click any links or download attachments, (3) Report the scam to cybercrime.gov.in, (4) Verify through official government websites listed in our report, (5) Warn others who may have received the same message.
